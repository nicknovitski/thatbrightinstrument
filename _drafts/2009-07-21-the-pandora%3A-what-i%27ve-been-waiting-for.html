If I had a nickel for every time I considered one of Nintendo's portable gaming consoles and said "What I really want is something that can fit in my pocket, and play Super Nintendo games," then I would have an indeterminate amount of money.


So.  It clamshells open to reveal an 800x640 widescreen touchscreen LCD.  There are four buttons on the right, a directional pad on the left, two analog buttons in the middle, along with "Select" and "Start" and "Home,"

It can connect with basically any USB device.  Mice, gamepads, thumb-drives, cellphones, GPS units.  It can direct output along an S-Video cable to a monitor or television.  It has a headphone/speaker jack.
