(The above is a quote that prefaces Sir Richard Francis Burton's travelogue The City of the Saints, which I highly recommend.  Dick's a hoot, and as sympathetic a narrator of indigenous peoples as you'll find in his century.)

Dogs in the Vineyard is a game where you create young folks full of hope who go from frontier town to frontier town, inflicting their moral standards on others and being made strange by the journey.

Yesterday ended our trek through the village of Bath'eshta.  At four sessions, it was so complicated a town that I almost expect us to start that was an extreme town, but we had a good time, and felt so much more sure of our characters, we agreed to visit at least one more before moving on to In a Wicked Age.  Though I hope Jim will make the next town slightly more straightforward; Dogs is as fun as ever, but I don't want to wait another two month for the next new game.   I crave variety in my diet.

My character, Job, developed strangely. Perhaps it was the lateness of the hour, or having begun to read Burton's travelogue, but he's become an unrepentant reaver of men for the sake of making the faithful live the way his clever-but-ignorant ways think they should. This passage struck me the most forcefully:



Yes, much like that.
