Now it is thought to be the mark of a man of practical wisdom to be able to deliberate well about what is good and expedient for himself, not in some particular respect, e.g. about what sorts of things conduce to good health or to strength, but about what sorts of thing conduce to the good life in general.
Aristotle, The Nicomachean Ethics

We are looking for a few good men.
U.S. Marine Corps Recruiting Poster
In other words, when you join a group, it's both because you think being in that group will be in your interest, and because the current members think that admitting you is in their interest.

The first section of the chapter concerns itself with the first half of this equation.

Individuals start, join, remain in and leave gangs for a variety of reasons, basing their decisions on a rational calculation of what is best for them at that particular time.

  1. Material Incentives
  2. Recreation
  3. A Place of Refuge and Camouflage
  4. Physical Protection
  5. A Time to Resist
  6. Commitment to Community.
