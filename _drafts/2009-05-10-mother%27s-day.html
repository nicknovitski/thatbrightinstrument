As I'm fond of saying, Confucius once said that you could carry your parents on your back for a thousand years and still not pay them back for what they've done for you.

On the other hand, once I said (and it was only once and I was only playing devil's advocate it's the opposite of my genuine opinion I swear) to a teacher that maybe they should, while doing their job, bear in mind the link between their salary and the tuition the students' parents pay.

This teacher, with good reason, disagreed vociferously.

In both cases, parents provide the material cause for the person's livelihood: the figurative pound of flesh for the teacher and the less-figurative for the child.  You can see how, in both cases, one might argue that something of equivalent value to that livelihood is needed to balance things out.  So that's why Confucius says that no finite amount of labor can match the value of being brought from non-existence to existence, the infinite jump from 0 to 1.
