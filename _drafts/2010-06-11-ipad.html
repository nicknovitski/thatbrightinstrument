--- layout: post title: iPad date: '2010-06-11T15:33:00.001-04:00' author: Nick Novitski tags: modified_time: '2010-06-15T13:06:18.173-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-31707300.post-1671888910920726506 --- I don't want an iPad.  I don't really want there to even be an iPad.

One silver lining of its existence is that I don't have to spend much time describing it, but if you can't imagine why I'd be down on it, it's that it's a machine designed only to enable consumption.  You can't print from it.  You can't connect.  You can neither create nor compute to any degree I consider sufficient.

But!  You can buy books (just don't try to share or take notes in them)!  You can buy movies (just don't try to watch them in a group)!  You can buy all kinds of assorted programs (just don't try to make your own to share with your friends).

The iPad is like a portable version of a corporate info-kiosk.  You can do only what they'll let you.




Yeah.  I don't like TVs much either.

Don't tell me that I don't understand the appeal of the iPad.  I understand it perfectly!  I feel it keenly!  Neophiles like us don't just like new things, new stuff, new expensive objects, don't people want to live in the future.

Here's a tenet we've tended to live by, that we can always benefit from re-stating:

Comport yourself as if you are a traveller from the near-future you most want to come true.
