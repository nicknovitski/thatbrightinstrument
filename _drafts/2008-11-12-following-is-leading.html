--- layout: post title: Following is Leading date: '2008-11-12T12:27:00.000-05:00' author: Nick Novitski tags: - neologism modified_time: '2008-11-12T14:42:26.365-05:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-31707300.post-5502580230814747935 --- So as part of their commitment to the next-generation open social network ecosystem, Blogger has added a feature where you can "follow" other feeds.  This is much like a friends list on livejournal, except divorced from the concept of a "friend": a feed can be a person you know, or just someone you think is entertainingly crazy, or updates for software that you use, or whatever.  All updates for these feeds appear at the bottom of your blogger dashboard, much like a cramped and less useful friends page.  Luckily, they are also automatically imported to Google Reader under the tag "Blogs I follow", and reader is a feed aggregating interface I find amenable.

The advantage of "following" is that it can be done publicly.  Not only can I include a gadget showing my allegiances (an abbreviated list, in my case, since I follow nearly 200 feeds and blogs) but those.

This latter feature currently only works for blogger users, but work it does: I can follow people, and when they see me following, they can decide whether what I say is of interest to them, and begin following me.
