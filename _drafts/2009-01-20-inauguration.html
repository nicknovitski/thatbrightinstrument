Long ago in China, there was a village on the wrong side of a mountain.

Don't get me wrong; it was a very beautiful spot, and the people who lived there were as happy and friendly as you'd expect them to be.  They liked their side of the mountain just fine, but the problem was that everywhere else of consequence was on the other side.  That meant that whenever anyone in the village wanted to go to market, or visit relatives, or go on pilgrimage or travel for any other reason, they always had to either climb all the way over the mountain or walk all the way around it.

No one liked having to do this, and some people moved to other villages on the right side.  But there were still some people who stayed.  They all accepted that the mountain was where it was, and they lived where they lived, and that was just the way things were going to be.

They all accepted that, except for one old man.
