The movie is not without it's flaws, but that's always been my favorite line from it, hands down.  Screw Donald Trump for over-writing that tiny corner of the zeitgeist with his enormous, useless bulk.

It's funny: losing your job means you suddenly have a lot of work to do.

Before the condolences come rushing in, I think I may have deserved it: how many times, in the past few days, have I complained about my commute?

More seriously, the bottom has certainly dropped out, and the future is far less certain.  Hooray!

Less seriously, just remember: in future, don't ever ask me where I was when we elected our first black president.

Edit:  Just to be totally clear, I was laid off, along with the other recent hires.  We were retained in the hopes of expanding the company's business, but with the recession (and yes, that's what it is), all of our clients simultaneously became much more reluctant to make outlays for web design and development work, and cuts had to be made.  It's a very regrettable situation, but I have nothing against the people who made the decision, who I still consider friends.  All of my superiors have offered me references, and I've taken up one of them on it.  I greatly value the time I spent at Blue Water Media for giving me immense experience, exposure to all kinds of skills and practices, and also for being a great time.  Not long before it happened, I confessed to my supervisor that this was by far the best job I've had yet.  I haven't revised that opinion, but I look forward to trying to match or exceed the high-water mark thus set on my next job!  Until then, I'm going to try to write, read, and also do some house chores I keep failing to finish.
