--- layout: post title: First Android Phone is as Hot as It Wanna Be date: '2008-09-24T21:00:00.000-04:00' author: Nick Novitski tags: - gadget - then I had some toast modified_time: '2009-06-01T22:24:20.257-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-31707300.post-8972330515559378960 blogger_orig_url: http://brightinstrument.blogspot.com/2008/09/first-android-phone-is-as-hot-as-it.html --- T-Mobile has announced that it will be releasing the world's first phone running the Android OS/framework/standard/stack, the T-Mobile G1.  I think they missed an opportunity to call it Torg. 

It will be released October 22nd, priced $179 with a two-year contract, unlimited data for $25 a month, and will be five thousand times sexier than the iPhone, for a bunch of mundane reasons, at the heart of which lies the simple fact: you own it and can change it and add to it and make it into exactly the device that you want, and Google will help you do it. 

I would buy it in a second, but it requires a 3G network, and T-Mobile has yet to start one in DC.

But!  Of course, if a way arose to "jailbreak" the phone, meaning that I could use it with a plan from Verizon, who does offer local 3G...

...then I could spend a bunch of money on something super-amazingly-awesome I don't need.  Best to take any excuse to wait and see how it all turns out.  I am following Android's progress with exceedingly keen interest.  I suspect it's going to graduallly and gently knock the iPhone onto its candy ass.
