It is a mystery!): The Genetic Society of America's Annual Novitski Prize.
The Novitski Prize, named in honor of Drosophila geneticist Edward Novitski (1918-2006), is designed to recognize an extraordinary level of creativity and intellectual ingenuity in solving significant problems in genetics research. The award recognizes scientific achievement that stands out from the body of innovative work, that is deeply impressive to creative masters in the field, and that solves a difficult problem that may have evaded the genetics scientific community. It recognizes the beautiful and intellectually ingenious experimental design and execution involved in genetics scientific discovery.
He published over a hundred papers and three books. The two more interesting ones are, at closest, to be found in the University of Maryland and the Library of Congress, but the famous one, The Genetics and Biology of Drosophila (fruit flies, to those not in the genetic know), is in the college library. Getting it now.
Genetics researchers using Drosophila and other model organisms have, over the years, conceived of and executed experiments that are monuments to an exceptional caliber of intellectual creativity that were impressive to colleagues at the time, rewarding to the researchers themselves through the joy of cracking a difficult problem, and are still impressive today. It is important to recognize the level of beautiful, intellectually ingenious, and creative experimental design and experimental execution. You know when you see it, when a master in the field says something like, “Wow, how did they ever figure out how to do that; it’s beautiful.” (For examples of this, see Novitski 2005, pp. 132-137.)
Well, I would certainly like to see examples of this in "Novitski 2005." It looks like an interesting work.

There's a game I read (and will soon be playing) that seeks to model the fiction of Wuxia, Heroic/Mythic China. As in so many Role-playing games, there are points which can be spent to increase the heroes skills and capabilities. Semi-uniquely, they can also be spent to tie the hero to the world in some way, which is why they are called "Destiny." One of the things that can be purchased is a strong descent, "by blood or martial tradition," (emphasis mine) from some famous or infamous folk from history.

It's likely I'm not related to this person. "Novitski" means "new people" in Polish, and it was the name given to any families who migrated over the eastern border unable to speak enough Polish to communicate effectively what their real name was. Descent by blood is impossible, but descent by martial tradition is a matter of character and choice, and I think I might want to choose it.

They say you can't choose your relatives. They're full of it. If you don't like the people around you, nothing is keeping you there besides you. Leave, whether in spirit or in the physical as well, and find someplace more to your liking, full of people you'd prefer to dimly reflect, or better, to brilliantly improve upon.

"Descent" is a misnomer, an artifact of medieval thought, that what was separated from us by the past was as glorious as our present was drab, that things could only descend, degenerate, as time went on.

From now on, only Ascend.
