--- layout: post title: Doughnut Day date: '2009-06-05T17:30:00.020-04:00' author: Nick Novitski tags: - then I had some toast modified_time: '2009-06-05T17:30:00.577-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-31707300.post-655976728896520984 blogger_orig_url: http://brightinstrument.blogspot.com/2009/06/doughnut-day.html --- The first Friday of June is consecrated to the sacred memory of the deep-fried batter-torus.  I was going to celebrate it by eating my first non-shitty doughnut in a great many months, but I couldn't find any nearby.  I can't stand these inflated, slimy things that most vendors seem eager to pass of as a pleasant treat.  A proper doughnut has a texture like a dry, crispy muffin, not a ropey fungal souffl√©.  The Devil take the man who invented "glazing" as it is currently practiced!  Also, what's with spelling it "donut?"  Why not just call it "fryd bred" and have done?  And don't even get me started on "Krispy Kreme."

Honestly, I'm pleased with there being a holiday for the doughnut.  Days of remembrance frequently annoy me for seeming to marginalize important things.  Black History needs constant discovery and integration, not its own month.  Labor should rule the world, not be given one day in September for drinking beer and being used for about when to wear white.  And that's leaving aside the downright offensive instances like Columbus Day, or historically-skewed presentations of Thanksgiving and Christmas.

The doughnut is a small joy, and having just one day a year to celebrate it seems proportional to its importance.
