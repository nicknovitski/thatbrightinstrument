Part 1 was my form of gushing, and it was right to start this series with giving the immediate context: for a large number of people, including myself, Blizzard is awesome.

But problems deserve to be the first thing addressed.  I wouldn't feel right putting this segment (and the next one, Race) at the end of the sequence, as if they were appendices.  These sorts of thoughts are central to my perceptions of Blizzard properties, whether they like it or not.
So, it was revealed a year or so ago that Diablo 3, for the first time in the series, would let you choose between male and female avatars for each class.  This was striking.

Before this announcement, when the (male) Barbarian class was unveiled, I gave a whoop of joy.  He's old.  This is Phoenix on the Sword Conan meets The Light Fantastic Cohen.  He may very well be the same Barbarian of Diablo 2, thirty or forty years on, having failed to die so many times in such incredible circumstances that he's no longer sure if he knows how.  He should be raising barns to house his children, but the call for heroes finds him on the side of a mountain, roasting a bear, and he does not even consider not facing the KINGS OF THE DEVILS yet again.



Sweeeet...

Then the female Barbarian was revealed, and I was even more overjoyed.



It has been said that pop culture's portrayal of "bad-ass" girls ranges from full-bodied objectification to spritely paedophilia, and that they should look more like this female shot-put champion:


And the female Barbarian is that.  She's not perfect: a slight she-hulk vibe persists, with the big hair and the dominatrix sexuality and everything.  But she's built and dressed as unapologetically as her male counterpart,  to crush her enemies, see them driven before her, and...well, the point is, she's an Awesome Woman Kicking Ass, just like the game!

I still mean to play that one...

And yes, let's switch games for a moment.

The tall and lissome Tricia Helfer was announced as the voice talent portraying Kerrigan in Starcraft 2.  This was revealed with mild fanfare, and a milder reaction.

Helfer was catapulted to the heights nerd-recognizability for portraying the the character of Number 6 on Battlestar Galactica, a crazed sexpot hallucination, a psychotic inhuman thing that takes over your life, won't stop talking about children and being together forever, which you're always either fucking or running from in terror.

So no, not the most progressive of roles.  Is Kerrigan an improvement?

Maybe.

See, there were three female characters in Starcraft: the unnamed dropship pilots (you know, like in Aliensno other intellectual property I know of), the unnamed Valkyrie (get it?!) fighter-jet pilots, and Sarah Kerrigan, special ops badass, abandoned by her allies to the evil aliens, shaped into a weapon of fear and horror, who betrayed and consumed her own hive-mind and became the post-human leader of a non-human army.

(Amusingly, this later became the exact same story of Arthas the Lich King, current big bad of World of Warcraft.  Behind every male boss-monster...)

Oh, and before someone bothers to correct me, the game's expansion, Brood War, added a third female: the Medic, also unnamed, who had no weapon, but just followed the testosterone-dripping marines around at a respectful distance, supporting and tending to them.  Yeah, I know.

Anyway, Brood War ends with Kerrigan's apotheosis, which comes after a rejection of the one person (okay, it's a man) who might have convinced her to not try to consume the universe.  Paraphrasing, her parting shot is "Gentlemen, prepare to be fucked."  She's another smothering, all-encompassing trans-female villain, in the vein of System Shock's SHODAN.

They've made her a life-size statue, which was at the convention, complete with glowing eyes, wing-like scythe claws, and shapely ass.  I refused to take a picture of it, because I don't masturbate to The Guyver with a reptile-skin glove, and but I did take this picture of a piece in the art gallery which, I think, depicts her in a later stage of "evolution" (boy do sci-fi games love to misuse that word).



What an awesome picture!  She is unlike any other alien form, and is holding a skull and...contemplating?  Did she know him, Horatio?  Does she remember Kerrigan?  Does she ever regret her irreversible path of vengeance?  Forget the pre-civilized Barbarian.  This Kerrigan is post-civilized, post-biological...post-everything!  An egotist so driven and single-minded that the universe may break before she does.  She's Foyle from The Stars My Destination meets Dave from 2001.

But this is my wishful thinking.  I don't really know if that's Kerrigan, or some other unannounced character.  In statue form, in all the cutscenes and promotional pictures, she is slim and athletic, yet quite feminine.  Why?  Because at least 80% of Blizzard's customers are male, and the other 20% are their wives and girlfriends.  Because one of the eight principles is "Embrace your inner geek," and in genderless English, "geek" is a clearly masculine word.
