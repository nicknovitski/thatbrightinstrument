Now that your essay writing period is over, we write to give you information about your oral examinations. Please review this memo and let us know if you have any questions.
As stated in our catalog, an approved senior essay requires an hour-long public examination by a committee of three tutors who have judged the essay to be acceptable. All senior essay oral examinations will be scheduled outside of class hours so that you (and your classmates) will be able to attend orals without missing class.
      Place: All senior oral examinations take place in the King William Room in the Barr Buchanan Center. Prior to the exam, you will don your cap and gown in the Robing Room across from the King William Room.
      Dates: Monday, February 18 through Thursday, February 28 (prior to Spring Break); and
Monday, March 17 through Friday, May 2
What You Need to Do and Remember:
    1. So that visitors may have some idea of your essayâ€™s content, you will begin by delivering a brief summary of your paper. Two minutes in length is a suitable time. Please read it very audibly, and continue to speak audibly throughout the oral. The examination is meant to be public, and the audience will appreciate being able to hear the conversation.

    1. The most successful orals are those that develop most like a conversation. As in a conversation, you are entitled to take the initiative if you feel the discourse flagging.

Protocol: We have attached the oral examination protocol. Please read it so that you are familiar with the procedure.
    1. Members of the audience must arrive before the time scheduled for the examination. No one should enter the King William Room after the oral has begun or before it ends except in an emergency.
    2. The examining committee and the student who is to be examined gather in the robing room at least five minutes before the examination is to begin. A member of the committee reviews the protocol with the student.
    3. Two of the committee members enter the King William Room before the student, and the audience rises. These committee members take their places at the table and remain standing. The third member enters with the student and closes the door.
    4. The third committee member tips his (or her) hat to the Chair and says**:
      Mr. (or Madam) Chair, I present ___________, candidate for the degree of Bachelor of Arts to be examined on his (or her) essay entitled "_________________".
    5. The Chair asks everyone to be seated.
    6. The Chair asks the student for a brief summary of the essay, and (if necessary) ENCOURAGES EVERYONE TO SPEAK AUDIBLY.
  1. Promptly after one hour, the Chair announces the end of the examination. As the audience rises, the student, followed by the committee, leaves the King William Room and returns to the robing room. The student vacates the robing room as soon as possible so that the committee members may confer.
